# App Philosophy

Here you will find a bank of software that is carefully thought-through.

These are intentionally biased toward being dumb, single-focused software
applications for maximum usefulness.

There is a beauty to behold in an object that does one thing well enough.

As its usefulness is proven through continued use, the meaningful incremental
improvements to make tend to reveal themselves organically.

That may not be the end-all-be-all, but I believe this approach makes for
purposeful craft.

## Start from the problem, not the conventions

Every interface arrives with inherited defaults — a button to submit, a cursor
to blink, a label to explain. Working from first principles means asking of each
one: does this need to exist for the problem at hand? Most don't. What survives
is a tool that explains itself by where things sit and how they move — a UI you
shouldn't have to explain, the way a good joke shouldn't need explaining.

This only works because the apps stay small. Single focus is the forcing
function: when an app does one thing, every element can be held up against that
one thing, and only what earns its place remains.

The aim isn't to strip convention for its own sake — conventions are compressed
knowledge, and some earn their keep. It's to make each one prove it. And every
iteration is another chance to question one more.
