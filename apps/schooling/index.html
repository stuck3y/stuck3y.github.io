Schooling Decision One-Pager

Homeschool · Private · Public — a tool for you and your spouse to pray and think through together
Deuteronomy 6:6–7 — "These words that I command you today shall be on your heart. You shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way, and when you lie down, and when you rise."

Conviction & Calling

  • Is the Lord directing us toward one path?
  • Are we choosing from fear, pride, or convenience?
  • Have we prayed together about this specifically?
  • Are we united as husband and wife on the direction?

The Child

  • Thrives in structure/social settings or one-on-one?
  • Academic level: behind, on-track, gifted, special needs?
  • Spiritual maturity — grounded or still being formed?
  • Friendships and influences they'd be around?

The Parents

  • Do we have time, patience, temperament to homeschool well?
  • Marriage bandwidth — is this a season we can take this on?
  • Financial reality — what can we actually afford?
  • Are we equipped to disciple through what they'll face?

The Schools Available

  • Public: actual quality of our local school, not the idea
  • Private: genuinely Christ-centered or just "Christian-branded"?
  • What is the school's doctrine, leadership, fruit?
  • Local homeschool co-ops, hybrids, classical options?

The Honest Trade-offs

Public

Gains
Free, exposure, diverse socialization, parents keep margin
Costs
Worldview formation outside our control, time with peers > time with us, content we can't vet

Private Christian

Gains
Aligned worldview (if real), structured rigor, like-minded community
Costs
Tuition, possible insularity or entitlement, "Christian" branding ≠ Christ-centered

Homeschool

Gains
Maximum discipleship, customizable pace, family-centered, full content control
Costs
Enormous parental investment, socialization must be intentional, isolation risk
The non-negotiable: There is no biblically-mandated schooling model — faithful Christians land in all three. But Deuteronomy 6 and Ephesians 6:4 are clear: you are the primary discipler. School is a tool, not a substitute. Whichever you choose, the discipleship at home is the load-bearing wall.
